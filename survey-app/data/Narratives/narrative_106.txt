On Wednesday, March 15, 2023, at [INSERT: time of incident], officers from Boston's B-2 District were on patrol in the area of Seaver Street when we observed a [INSERT: vehicle make and model] driving at a high rate of speed. Officers conducted a traffic stop. During the stop, all occupants failed to produce proper identification, so they were all ordered to exit the vehicle.

Upon searching the vehicle, officers recovered substances believed to be crack cocaine and fentanyl. It was later determined that police recovered roughly 41 grams of crack cocaine and 68 grams of fentanyl. Additionally, officers located a significant amount of cash, totaling $50,556 in U.S. currency. During the search, an officer remarked on the discovery of the money when a light was shined inside.

The driver was identified as Ray Lara, 28, of Hyde Park. During the interaction, I informed Mr. Lara that he was driving in a "crazy way." Another officer indicated he was moving at speeds up to 90. Mr. Lara stated, "Honestly, I thought I was going 30," but admitted he knew he was going fast and "slowed up." Mr. Lara remarked that his legs were shaking during the encounter. The other occupants stated they were visiting from Florida to see a cousin.

Mr. Lara was arrested on site and charged with trafficking of Class A, trafficking of Class B, and possession with intent to distribute Class D. Following the arrest, Mr. Lara requested that we leave the keys to his father's truck at the front desk for his father to retrieve. Mr. Lara was subsequently arraigned in Dorchester District Court.
