It was Thursday, April 25th, 2024.
It was approximately 2:00 a.m.
I was present at Emerson College.
I was alongside Captain Sean Martin.
Captain Sean Martin is of District A1.
I was alongside other officers.
My presence was regarding a protest encampment.
Captain Martin attempted to de-escalate the situation.
Captain Martin advised the protesters that the department supports their right to protest.
Captain Martin advised the protesters that the department did not want to arrest anyone.
Captain Martin advised the protesters that the "block in the street" could not be allowed.
Captain Martin explained that "fire codes" were being broken.
Captain Martin explained that a nearby building required emergency access.
Captain Martin explained that the department was receiving complaints from residents.
Captain Martin stated the complaints were being received because it was late.
Captain Martin informed the group that they were supposed to "come down about 10 p.m."
Captain Martin stated he had given the group several hours extra.
Captain Martin stated the extra time was to finish their finals.
Captain Martin stated the extra time was to protest peacefully without blocking the public way.
An officer utilized a sound system.
The officer read a warning regarding City Ordinance Section 16-65.
City Ordinance Section 16-65 is also known as the "Camping Ordinance."
The ordinance was passed on October 25, 2023.
The officer advised that the ordinance prohibits persons from camping in any public right of way.
The officer advised that the ordinance prohibits maintaining campsite materials in any public right of way.
The officer listed streets, sidewalks, schools, and public parks as examples of public rights of way.
The officer stated the prohibition applies unless specifically authorized during a period when shelter is unavailable.
The officer read protocols regarding offers of shelter.
The protocols state that officers would work with the coordinated response team to find placement if an individual accepts shelter.
The protesters continued to chant "Free, free Palestine."
The protesters did not disperse.
Officers took action to clear the public area of the protesters.
Officers took action to clear the public area of the encampment.
Commands were issued to "take the person with the backpack."
Commands were issued to "grab onto them."
Instructions were issued to move a vehicle.
Instructions were issued to "push the sidewalk back beyond the obedience."
Four officers were injured during the intervention.
One officer sustained serious injuries.
The serious injuries were non-life-threatening.
A total of 108 individuals were arrested.
The individuals are set to be arraigned in Boston Municipal Court.
The arraignments are set for either May 1st or May 8th.
Sergeant John Boyle confirmed the arrest and arraignment details.
