I observed a firearm on the person of a male subject.
The male subject was later identified as Mr. King.
I noted the weapon was real.
I noted the weapon was loaded.
The firearm was tucked in the subject's hoodie pocket.
I secured Mr. King one-handed.
I did not initially want to wake Mr. King up.
I instructed [INSERT: partner/other officer] to place Mr. King in handcuffs.
I asked Mr. King if he was "with it."
I asked Mr. King if he knew where he was.
I demanded to see Mr. King's license to carry a firearm.
Mr. King stated, "I never had a license."
I asked Mr. King if he had any idea what he was wearing.
Mr. King replied, "Yeah, I do."
I asked Mr. King for his name.
Mr. King stated, "I don't even know what."
I read Mr. King his Miranda rights.
I advised Mr. King that he had the right to remain silent.
I advised Mr. King that anything he said could be used against him in a court of law.
I advised Mr. King that he had the right to consult with a lawyer before answering questions.
I advised Mr. King that a lawyer would be provided by the Commonwealth if he could not afford one.
I asked Mr. King if he understood what I told him.
Mr. King answered, "No."
I acknowledged Mr. King's response.
I ordered for Mr. King to be removed from the scene.
Officers recovered the weapon.
The weapon was identified as a 9mm polymer 80PF940C.
The weapon contained three rounds in the magazine.
The weapon contained one round in the chamber.
Investigation revealed that Mr. King had a prior firearm conviction.
The prior firearm conviction occurred in 2015.
Mr. King was placed under arrest.
Mr. King was charged with possession of a firearm without an FID card (subsequent offense).
Mr. King was charged with possession of ammunition without an FID card.
Mr. King was charged with possession of a large capacity firearm.
Mr. King was processed for arraignment at Dorchester District Court.
